# Blacklist — English Patterns

Last updated: 2026-02-27

---

## LEVEL 1 — FORBIDDEN (eliminate 100%)

### Structural Patterns

- "It's not about X, it's about Y"
- "And here's the truth:"
- "And nobody is talking about it"
- "It's more than A, it's B"
- "Let that sink in"
- "Read that again"
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Here's what most people miss:"
- "This. This right here."
- "Let me be clear:"
- "Spoiler:" / "Plot twist:"
- "What if I told you..."
- "The question nobody asks:"
- "And that's okay."

### Dead Words

- **Delve** / **Dive deep** / **Unpack** — the #1 most detected AI word in English
- **Leverage** / **Harness** / **Navigate** — corporate AI speak
- **Embark on** / **Journey** — forced epic framing
- **Cutting-edge** / **Game-changer** — empty buzzwords
- **Tapestry** / **Landscape** / **Realm** — AI loves these metaphors
- **Multifaceted** / **Nuanced** — classic AI adjectives
- **Robust** / **Scalable** / **Synergy** — corporate jargon
- **Groundbreaking** — overused to meaninglessness
- **Seamless** / **Seamlessly** — AI default qualifier
- **Pivotal** — AI loves this word
- **Cornerstone** — architectural metaphor overuse
- **Fostering** — AI default verb for "encouraging"

### Forbidden Transitions

- Furthermore
- Moreover
- Additionally
- It's worth noting
- It's important to
- At the end of the day
- Moving forward
- In conclusion

### Forbidden Buzzwords

- Actionable insights
- Best practices
- Low-hanging fruit

- "That's a really interesting point"

### Forbidden Closings

- "Feel free to reach out"
- "Don't hesitate to"
- "Let me know if you have any questions"
- "Happy to help"

### Forbidden Adjectives

- Incredible

- Fantastic
- Remarkable

- Holistic

## LEVEL 2 — ALERT (substitute or rewrite)

### Suspicious Rhetorical Patterns

- Poeticizing technical subjects
- Unsolicited professorial tone
- Cascade of adjectives (3+ adjectives = AI)
- Repeating the same idea with synonyms
- Excessive neutrality (no opinion, no personality)
- Excessive hedging ("perhaps", "maybe", "it could be that", "in some ways")
- Construction metaphors ("building bridges", "foundation", "pillar", "cornerstone")

### Risky Expressions (red flag when combined)

- "The beauty of it is..."
- "At its core..."
- "The key takeaway..."
- "It boils down to..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "In essence..."
- "The bottom line is..."

## LEVEL 3 — MONITOR (detectable in combination)

### Words OK alone, but AI-flagged in combo

- Profound
- Authentic
- Genuine
- Impact
- Transformation
- Perspective
- Approach
- Dynamic
- Narrative
- Framework
- Ecosystem

### Subtle Patterns

- Conclusions restating the obvious
- Uniformly positive tone without nuance
- Total absence of imperfections
- Consistently "elevated" vocabulary without colloquial variation
- All sentences similar in length (no rhythm variation)

## TONE OF VOICE (English)

Same principles as Portuguese:
- Direct, to the point
- Short AND long sentences alternated (human cadence)
- Natural, conversational language
- Confident assertions
- Concrete examples with numbers
- Active voice always ("I did", not "it was done")
- Opinion > neutrality
- Strong nouns > empty adjectives
- Controlled imperfections > artificial perfection
- Normal punctuation (period, comma). No decorative em dashes
- Zero emojis
- Prose over bullet points when it makes sense
